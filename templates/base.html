Patient Resources

Everything you need to know before, during, and after your visit to our imaging center.

MRI Preparation

  • Wear comfortable, loose-fitting clothing without metal fasteners
  • Remove all metal objects (jewelry, watches, hair pins, etc.)
  • Inform staff of any implanted medical devices or metal in your body
  • Arrive 30 minutes before your scheduled appointment
  • For contrast studies, you may need to fast for 4-6 hours prior

CT Scan Preparation

  • Wear comfortable clothing without metal zippers or buttons
  • You may be asked to fast for 4 hours before your exam if contrast is used
  • Inform staff if you have allergies to iodine or contrast materials
  • Arrive 15 minutes before your scheduled appointment
  • Bring your doctor's referral and any previous imaging studies

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I prepare for an MRI?

For most MRI exams, no special preparation is needed. Wear comfortable clothing without metal fasteners. You'll need to remove all metal objects, including jewelry, watches, and hearing aids. For some exams, you may need to fast for 4-6 hours prior. Our staff will provide specific instructions when scheduling your appointment.

How long do imaging procedures take?

Procedure times vary depending on the type of exam. X-rays typically take 15-30 minutes, ultrasounds 30-45 minutes, CT scans 15-30 minutes, and MRIs 30-60 minutes. These times include preparation and the actual scan time.

When will I get my results?

Our radiologists interpret exams promptly, usually within 24-48 hours. The results are sent to your referring physician, who will discuss them with you. For urgent cases, results can be expedited and available the same day.

Is radiation from imaging procedures safe?

We follow the ALARA principle (As Low As Reasonably Achievable) for radiation exposure. Our modern equipment uses the lowest possible dose while maintaining diagnostic quality. MRI and ultrasound do not use radiation at all. The benefits of accurate diagnosis typically far outweigh the minimal risks.

Can I bring someone with me to my appointment?

Yes, you may bring a friend or family member for support. They can stay with you during registration and preparation, but may need to remain in the waiting area during the actual procedure, depending on the type of exam.
